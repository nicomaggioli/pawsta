Roast notes & cupping
04.30.26 — 12kg batch

Bright. Floral. Annoyingly good.

Sourced from the Konga washing station in the Yirgacheffe zone of southern Ethiopia, this lot of heirloom varietals was harvested in November and washed using the traditional 72-hour fermentation method.

We roast it light — first crack only — to preserve the volatile aromatics that make Ethiopian coffee taste like nothing else on earth. Expect jasmine on the nose, a syrupy bergamot body, and a long peach finish that sticks around for a while.

Cupped by our Q-grader, M. Ito. Score: 89.5/100. Notes recorded 04.28.26.

— A note from the farm

Two hundred small-
holders. One lot.

The Konga washing station processes coffee from over 200 smallholder farms within a 5-kilometer radius. Cherries are hand-sorted, depulped within 6 hours of harvest, and fermented in concrete tanks for 72 hours before sun-drying on raised beds.

We've been buying from Konga since 2021. We pay 30% above the C-market price, and we visit every other year.
